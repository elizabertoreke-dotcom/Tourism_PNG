Discover Papua New Guinea

Papua New Guinea (PNG) is a land of rich cultural diversity, dramatic highland scenery, pristine coral reefs, and world-class biodiversity. From traditional village sing-sings to rugged trekking and remarkable birdlife, PNG rewards travelers who seek authenticity and adventure.

Highlights: Sepik River, Kokoda Track, Tufi fjords, Tari Basin, and the Coral Triangle.

Top Destinations

Kokoda Track

The historic Kokoda Track offers a challenging multi-day trek through the Owen Stanley Range, combining WWII history with remote mountain wilderness and warm local hospitality.

Sepik River

Explore canoe villages, traditional carved spirit houses, and riverside markets along one of the most culturally rich river systems in the world.

Tufi – Fjords and Reefs

Tufi offers spectacular fjords, clear water diving, and eco-lodges tucked into dramatic coastal inlets.

Tari Basin & Birdwatching

Home to the famed birds-of-paradise, the Tari Basin is a must for birdwatchers and cultural encounters with highland communities.

Culture & People

The highlands are home to vibrant cultures with elaborate traditional dress, music, and dance. Festivals like the Mount Hagen Show showcase these rich traditions.

Explore the rich cultural heritage of Papua New Guinea through its diverse array of traditional artifacts, from intricate carvings to ceremonial objects.

Bilums are traditional handwoven bags that hold cultural significance in Papua New Guinea, often used for carrying goods and as a symbol of identity.

The binning fire dance is a traditional performance in Papua New Guinea that involves dancers performing around a fire, often during cultural festivals and ceremonies, showcasing the rich heritage of the region as well as keeping a strong bond with their ancestors.

Wildlife & Nature

Birds-of-paradise

PNG is the premier destination to see multiple species of birds-of-paradise in their natural habitats—an unforgettable wildlife highlight.

Marine biodiversity

Part of the Coral Triangle, PNG's reefs rank among the most biodiverse on Earth, with world-class diving and snorkeling opportunities.

Adventure Activities

Trekking

Beyond Kokoda, guided treks vary from village walks to multi-day expeditions in remote highlands.

Scuba & Snorkel

Liveaboards, house reefs, and coastal dive operators run trips around Tufi, Kimbe Bay, and Milne Bay.

Cultural Tours

Explore culturals from the mountains down to the islands with local guides who can share insights into customs, history, and daily life.

Practical Travel Tips

Safety

Exercise caution in cities; use reputable guides and tourism operators. Check travel advisories before planning.

Health

Vaccinations and malaria precautions are recommended depending on region. Carry a small medical kit and insect repellent.

Permits & Visas

Visitors usually require a visa; some regions may require special permits for trekking or protected areas—confirm with operators.

Plan Your Trip

Work with local operators for cultural experiences, ensure respectful photography practices, and allow extra time for internal travel—PNG's infrastructure can make journeys rewarding but slower-paced.
